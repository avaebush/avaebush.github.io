Background

I am an Earth and geospatial scientist with over ten years of research experience in environmental systems science and spatial analysis. My work focuses on understanding complex Earth processes and translating research insights into practical applications.

I specialize in bridging scientific research to applied contexts, with an emphasis on climate-related challenges, environmental change, and data-informed decision-making. I also prioritize accessibility in geospatial and environmental science more accessible and strive to ensure results are actionable for non-specialist audiences.

I am committed to community-driven climate education. I look forward to developing strategies that connect scientific knowledge to lived experience, expand access to STEM teaching and learning, and support more inclusive engagement with climate science.

Research

To address these challenges, I develop research among three interdisciplinary themes:

  • Climate impacts: Quantifying the effects of climate variability on ecosystem-geomorphology feedbacks.
  • Decision support: Designing spatiotemporal indicators to inform disaster response and anticipatory adaptation.
  • Resilience metrics: Supporting land management and natural resources solutions to resolve the cascading consequences of hydrological intensification.
