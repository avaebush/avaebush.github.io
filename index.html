Background

I am an Earth and geospatial scientist and educator specializing in the analysis of complex geological, climatic, and ecological systems and the translation of scientific research into applied solutions.

My work sits at the intersection of systems science, climate adaptation, and data-driven intelligence, with a strong emphasis on making research accessible and actionable among diverse audiences.

I am passionate about community climate education, developing approaches that connect scientific insight to lived experience, broaden access to STEM, and support more inclusive participation in Earth science.

Research

To address challenges in Earth systems science, I organize my work around three interdisciplinary themes. My approach connects research with experiential learning by creating opportunities for stakeholders to engage directly with environmental data, landscape processes, and decision-making.

  • Climate impacts: I quantify the effects of climate variability on ecosystem–geomorphology feedbacks. This work is paired with field-based analysis and learning modules focused on observing and measuring landscape responses to climate variability, linking theoretical and empirical models with system dynamics.
  • Decision support: I design spatiotemporal indicators to inform anticipatory adaptation and hazard response. This work supports community-engaged learning and collaboration around locally relevant decision-making.
  • Resilience metrics: I supporting land management and natural resource solutions to addressing the cascading consequences of hydrological intensification. My goal is to incorporate applied, project-based metrics that center geospatial analysis, data interpretation, and science communication.

Within these themes, I am developing research-to-implementation frameworks that integrate fieldwork, data-driven inquiry, and community engagement. This includes designing accessible curricula, expanding opportunities for Earth systems analysis, and fostering pathways for researchers from diverse backgrounds to participate. By blending education, outreach, and operationalization with physical science research, I intend to support inclusive and impactful approaches to climate, environmental, and hazard science.
