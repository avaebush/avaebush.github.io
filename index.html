Introduction

I am an Earth systems scientist investigating how climate change influences landscapes, ecosystems, and natural hazards. My research combines geospatial analysis, statistical modeling, artificial intelligence, and community-engaged science to develop evidence-based strategies for climate adaptation and resource governance. As an educator, I create learning experiences that connect science to environmental challenges through data analysis, quantitative reasoning, and collaborative inquiry.

Research

To address challenges in Earth systems science, I organize my work around three interdisciplinary themes. My approach connects research with experiential learning by creating opportunities to engage directly with environmental data, landscape processes, and decision science. Current projects:

  • AI for climate, ecosystem, and land evolution research
  • Climate-driven hazard analysis
  • STEM and community climate education
