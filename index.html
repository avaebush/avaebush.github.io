Background

I am an Earth and geospatial scientist and educator. My work focuses on understanding complex geological, climatic, and ecological processes while translating research insights into practical applications.

I specialize in bridging research to applied contexts, with an emphasis on systems science, climate adaptation, and data-informed solutions. I also prioritize accessibility and strive to ensure results are actionable for specialist and non-specialist audiences.

I am passionate about community-driven climate education. I look forward to developing strategies that connect scientific knowledge to lived experience, expand access to STEM teaching and learning, and support more inclusive engagement with Earth science.

Research

To address challenges in Earth systems science, I develop research among three interdisciplinary themes. My approach emphasizes connecting research with experiential learning by creating opportunities for communities to engage directly with environmental data, landscape processes, and decision science.

  • Climate impacts: Quantifying the effects of climate variability on ecosystem–geomorphology feedbacks. This work is paired with field-based learning modules that allow students to observe and measure landscape responses to climate variability, linking theoretical models with system dynamics.
  • Decision support: Designing spatiotemporal indicators to inform disaster response and anticipatory adaptation. My goal is to incorporate applied, project-based learning in which students engage with real datasets and develop decision-support tools, building skills in geospatial analysis, data interpretation, and science communication.
  • Resilience metrics: Supporting land management and natural resource solutions to address the cascading consequences of hydrological intensification. This theme provides a foundation for community-engaged learning and collaboration on locally relevant resilience strategies.

Within these themes, I am developing a research-to-education frameworks that integrate fieldwork, data-driven inquiry, and community engagement. This includes designing accessible curricula, expanding opportunities for Earth systems analysis, and fostering pathways for students from diverse backgrounds to participate in research. By blending education with research, I intend to support inclusive and impactful approaches to climate and environmental science.
